Appetizer

Savoring traditional Japanese cuisine begins with a carefully crafted appetizer.

酢の物 (Sunomono)

A refreshing taste – a perfect balance of sweet and sour flavors

Variety – often featuring seaweed, cucumber, or seafood

Lightness – a healthy and palate-cleansing option

漬の物 (Tsukemono)

Salty and crunchy – offering a satisfying texture

A regular side dish – commonly served with meals

Flavorful – infused with umami-rich seasonings

枝豆 (Edamame)

Simply prepared – boiled in salt water

Nutritious – rich in protein and fiber

Delicious – naturally savory with a satisfying bite

茶碗蒸し (Chawanmushi)

Japanese style steamed egg smooth and soft, packed with shrimp, chicken, ginkgo and other seasonings.

金平牛蒡 (Kinpira Gobo)

Sautéed with shredded burdock and carrots and the sauce is rich in flavor and dietary fiber.

冷奴 (Hiyayakko)

Cold tofu drizzled with soy sauce and served with green onions, sliced woodchuck, and mashed ginger for a refreshing flavor.
