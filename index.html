Cuisine Culture in Showa Era

Nostalgic Taste and Memory

topic

The Showa era (1926-1989) marked a period of profound transformation in Japan, blending rich traditions with rapid modernization and growth.

topic

The Showa era (1926-1989) marked a period of profound transformation in Japan, blending rich traditions with rapid modernization and growth.

Appetizer

In the Showa era, simple pickled vegetables like tsukemono or lightly salted edamame were often served as appetizers.

Main Course

Grilled fish, such as mackerel or salmon, paired with rice and miso soup, was a staple main course during this time.

Soup

Miso soup, made with tofu, seaweed, and green onions, was a daily essential in Showa-era meals.

Dessert

Traditional desserts like anmitsu, a jelly-based treat with sweet red beans, were enjoyed as a light and refreshing end to the meal.
